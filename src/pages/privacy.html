Privacy

Privacy & our no-data-selling pledge

Here's the short version: we don't want your personal information, so we don't collect it. There's no form on this site, no account to create, and no phone number to give us. The tools do their math right on your device, and the numbers you type never reach us.

What we don't collect

Most websites for people your age exist to capture a name and a phone number and pass it to a call center. BenefitClock was built to be the opposite of that. We do not collect:

  • Your name. There's nowhere to enter it.
  • Your address. We don't ask where you live. When you pick a state or county in the Medicare tool, that choice is used only to look up public plan data in your browser — it isn't tied to you.
  • Your email. There's no sign-up, no newsletter, and no "enter your email to see results."
  • Your phone number. Ever. There is no "talk to a licensed agent," no callback request, and no one will call you because you visited this site.
  • Any account or login. There are no passwords because there are no accounts.

The calculators run entirely in your browser

When you type your Social Security benefit into the COLA calculator or choose your plan in the What Changed tool, the math happens on your own device. Those numbers are never sent to us, never stored on a server, and never used for advertising. Close the tab and they're gone.

What is collected automatically

We want to be honest and complete, so here is the small amount of information that is gathered when anyone visits a modern website like this one.

Basic, aggregate web analytics

Like most sites, we use standard analytics to understand how many people visit and which pages are useful — for example, how many people opened the COLA calculator this week. This is looked at in aggregate (totals and trends), not as a profile of you. It helps us decide what to build and fix. It does not include your name, and we don't use it to identify you personally.

Our display-ad provider

BenefitClock is free and is supported only by programmatic display ads — the banner and box ads you see on the page. Those ads are served by a third-party advertising network (for example, an ad partner such as Google AdSense, Mediavine, or Raptive). To show ads and measure whether they worked, that network may set its own cookies or use similar identifiers in your browser. That is standard across the web, and we're telling you plainly because you deserve to know.

You are in control of how personalized those ads are. You can review and adjust your choices here:

  • Digital Advertising Alliance — WebChoices ad controls
  • Google Ads Settings (if you have a Google account)
  • Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page

We don't control the ad network's own privacy practices, but we chose the display-ad model specifically because it doesn't require us to know who you are. We are paid when ads are shown — not when your information is collected or sold.

What we will never do

This is the heart of our pledge. BenefitClock is not a broker, an agent, or a marketing company. We take no commissions from insurance carriers, we capture no personal information, and we sell nothing but ad space. So we will never:

  • Sell or share your personal data. We don't have your name, email, or phone number to sell in the first place — and we've built the site that way on purpose.
  • Generate or sell sales leads. You are a reader, not a lead. There is no list, and nothing about you is packaged and sold.
  • Hand your information to a call center or an insurance broker. No one is standing by. When you're ready to enroll, you go straight to the official Medicare Plan Finder or call 1-800-MEDICARE. We never recommend a specific plan for payment.

Our promise in one line

We make money from ads on the page, never from you or your information. That's the whole business model — and it's why we can put your interests first.

Cookies

A cookie is a small file a website can store in your browser. We keep ours to a minimum. Any cookies used for basic analytics or by our ad network are described above. You can delete cookies or block them at any time in your browser's settings — the tools on this site will still work, because they don't depend on cookies to do their math.

Children

BenefitClock is intended for adults, primarily people approaching or already in retirement. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18.

Your choices

You have simple, practical control over your privacy here:

  • Do Not Track. If your browser sends a "Do Not Track" signal, that's fine — we don't build advertising profiles of you regardless.
  • Ad personalization. Use the ad-control links in the section above to limit how personalized the ads you see are.
  • Cookies. Clear or block cookies from your browser menu whenever you like.
  • Just leave. Because we never collected your name or contact details, there's nothing you need to "delete" or "unsubscribe" from. There's no account to close.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change how the site works in a way that affects your privacy, we'll update this page and change the "Last updated" date at the top. What will not change is the core promise: we don't collect your personal information, and we don't sell it.

Contact

Questions about privacy? You can reach us at privacy@benefitclock.org. Please note that because we don't operate a call center and aren't a broker, we can't look up your specific Medicare plan or Social Security record — for that, contact the official sources at Medicare.gov, the Social Security Administration, or 1-800-MEDICARE.

Frequently asked questions

Do you store the benefit amount I type into the calculator?

No. The entire calculation runs inside your web browser. There's no server that receives your benefit amount, and we never use it for advertising or connect it to you. See the COLA calculator to try it.

Will visiting this site lead to sales calls or junk mail?

No. We don't collect your phone number, address, or email, and we don't hand anything to a call center or broker. Nothing about your visit turns into a call or a piece of mail.

How does a free site pay its bills, then?

Programmatic display ads — the banner and box ads on the page. That's the only way we're funded. We take no carrier or broker commissions and sell no leads or data. Read more on How it works.

Can I stop seeing personalized ads?

Yes. Use the DAA WebChoices tool or your Google Ads Settings to limit ad personalization. You can also clear cookies from your browser at any time.

Independent by design.

Our privacy promise isn't a marketing line — it's built into how the whole site works. No forms, no phone number, no data selling.
