How to read your result

Every fall, CMS publishes the Landscape and Crosswalk files that spell out what happens to every Medicare Advantage and Part D plan. We turn those public files into a plain-English comparison. Your plan will fall into one of these categories:

  • Renewing — your plan continues, but the premium, deductible, or benefits may have moved. Check the table.
  • Consolidated — CMS is merging your plan into a successor plan. Your coverage maps to the new plan ID shown.
  • Service-area reduction — the plan is shrinking where it's offered. Confirm it's still available in your county.
  • Terminating — the plan won't be offered next year. You'll need to choose a new plan during Open Enrollment or risk being moved to Original Medicare.

Frequently asked questions

Where do I find my plan ID?

It's on your plan member ID card and on the Annual Notice of Change (ANOC) your plan mails every September. It looks like H1234-001 (or S/R followed by four digits for some plans). You can also just pick your plan by name from the dropdown.

Is this the official government tool?

No — and we say so proudly. BenefitClock is an independent utility. We use the same public CMS data the government publishes, but we're not affiliated with Medicare or CMS. For enrollment, always use the official Medicare Plan Finder or call 1-800-MEDICARE.

Will you ask for my phone number or sell my information?

Never. This tool doesn't collect your name, phone number, or email — there's no form to fill in and nothing is sent to a call center. That's the whole point of BenefitClock. Read our privacy pledge.

Why don't I see 2027 numbers yet?

CMS releases the {{COLA_PROJECTED_YEAR}} Landscape and Crosswalk files in late September or early October. Until the official files are published, this tool runs on a structurally identical sample dataset so you can see exactly how the comparison works. The moment CMS posts the real files, we load them and the numbers become live. See How it works.
