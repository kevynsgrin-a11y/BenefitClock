How the COLA and Part B work together

Each year, Social Security benefits rise by a cost-of-living adjustment (COLA) tied to inflation. But the headline percentage isn't the whole story. Two things determine what you actually keep:

  • The gross raise — your benefit multiplied by the COLA. A {{COLA_PROJECTED}}% COLA on a $2,000 benefit adds about $72 a month before anything is taken out.
  • Your Medicare Part B premium — for most people this is deducted straight from the Social Security check. When Part B rises, it quietly claws back part of the raise. In some years, a higher Part B premium has eaten up half of a retiree's COLA increase.

The one formula that matters

Net benefit = (your current benefit × (1 + COLA)) − your Medicare Part B premium. That's the number our calculator shows in the "After Part B is taken out" box.

Frequently asked questions

When is the {{COLA_PROJECTED_YEAR}} COLA officially announced?

The Social Security Administration announces the official COLA right after the Bureau of Labor Statistics releases September inflation data. For the {{COLA_PROJECTED_YEAR}} adjustment, that release is scheduled for {{COLA_ANNOUNCE_DATE_LONG}} at 8:30 a.m. ET. Until then, the {{COLA_PROJECTED}}% figure is an early projection, not a guarantee.

How is the COLA actually calculated?

By law, the COLA equals the percentage increase in the average CPI-W (the Consumer Price Index for Urban Wage Earners and Clerical Workers) for July, August, and September of this year compared with the same three months of the last year a COLA took effect — rounded to the nearest tenth of a percent. We walk through a worked example on our How it works page.

Why does my raise look smaller after Part B?

Because Medicare Part B is usually withheld from your Social Security payment. If your gross benefit goes up 3.6% but your Part B premium also rises, the increase to your deposit is smaller than the headline COLA. That gap is exactly what this tool makes visible.

Do you store the numbers I enter?

No. The entire calculation runs in your web browser. We don't have a server that receives your benefit amount, we don't use it for advertising, and we never ask for your name, email, or phone number. See our privacy pledge.
